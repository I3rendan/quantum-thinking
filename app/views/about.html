Anne Keough Keehn

Founder and CEO

QT was founded in 2002 as a practice and forum that would grow and evolve overtime to help serve the higher education community globally. Ms. Keehn has had a long and diverse career in higher education globally and is considered a visionary, thought leader and accomplished executive in growing and scaling technology initiatives, programs and companies that span across the areas of administration, teaching and learning, and research in higher education, K-12 and corporate learning. She most recently served as an Entrepreneur-in-Residence and Senior Fellow for Technology & Innovation for the Postsecondary Success Team for the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.

She has held several global leadership positions such as President, Global Solutions, Americas for Kaplan Ventures; SVP of Pearson Learning Solutions & Pearson eCollege; SVP for Corporate Strategy, Innovation, New Product Development and Marketing for SCT/SunGard HE (now Ellucian) and was the early stage Executive VP of Global Sales and Client Relations at Blackboard. Prior to joining Blackboard, Ms. Keehn was Vice President of HE Sales for PeopleSoft (now Oracle) and a Sales Director for Datatel (Ellucian). She also served as Director of Development for Prescott College and as an executive for Fujitsu Telecommunications and Cable & Wireless.

Susan E. Metros

Senior Consultant

Susan E. Metros is an information technology and academic innovation consultant and a professional coach and career mentor. She currently serves as Associate Dean for the University of Southern California’s Jimmy Iovine and Andre Young Academy for Arts, Technology and the Business of Innovation and Director for Remote Learning Programs in the USC Roski School of Art and Design. She was the former Interim Chief Information Officer for the USC Marshall School of Business and previously served as USC’s Associate Vice Provost for Technology Enhanced Learning and Associate CIO for USC’s Information Technology Services. She holds faculty appointments in the Roski School and in the USC Rossier School of Education and the USC Annenberg School of Communication and Journalism.

Susan offers over 20 years executive-level, higher education leadership experience and 35 years of university faculty experience. Her IT leadership portfolio includes CIO experience and oversight of both information and educational technology organizations, operations and services. She has deep knowledge in the areas of academic innovation, educational technology, learning space design, web services, and user experience. She is an expert on the full continuum of online learning from conceptualizing a program to launching a degree.
