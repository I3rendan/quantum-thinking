Anne Keough Keehn

Meet the Founder and CEO

Anne Keough Keehn is the Founder and CEO of Quantum Thinking.

QT was founded as a practice to advise and serve leaders within the higher education community - globally. Ms. Keehn is considered a visionary, thought leader and accomplished executive in growing and scaling technology initiatives, programs and companies. Her cross-sector experience spans across education technology, teaching and learning innovation, and research for higher education, K-12 and corporate learning.

She most recently served as an Entrepreneur-in-Residence and Senior Fellowfor Technology & Innovation for the Postsecondary Success Team for the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. Throughout her career, Ms. Keehn has held several global leadership positions such as President, Global Solutions, Americas for Kaplan Ventures; SVP of Pearson Learning Solutions & Pearson eCollege; SVP for Corporate Strategy, Innovation, New Product Development and Marketing for SCT/SunGard HE (now Ellucian) and was the early stage Executive VP of Global Sales and Client Relations at Blackboard. Prior to joining Blackboard, Ms. Keehn was Vice President of HE Sales for PeopleSoft (now Oracle) and a Sales Director for Datatel (Ellucian). She has also served as Director of Development and Alumni Relations for Prescott College in Arizona.

Ms. Keehn earned a B.S. English, from Towson University in Maryland. She has been published and speaks globally about education technology, design thinking, and academic innovation and change. She is passionate about improving higher education - student success, teaching and learning, faculty support, research and administration so more students can succesfully achieve their postseconday education goals.

Pro bono, Ms. Keehn is furthering the work she helped shape during her time at the Gates foundation by teaming up with other higher education leaders, academics, non-profits, associations and foundations on two projects: a national call for collaboration and further action for "Redesigning Student Advising & Support for Postsecondary Education" and co-launching the "Leading Academic Innovation & Change Network."

Susan E. Metros

Senior Consultant

Susan E. Metros is an information technology and academic innovation consultant and a professional coach and career mentor. She currently serves as Associate Dean for the University of Southern California’s Jimmy Iovine and Andre Young Academy for Arts, Technology and the Business of Innovation and Director for Remote Learning Programs in the USC Roski School of Art and Design. She was the former Interim Chief Information Officer for the USC Marshall School of Business and previously served as USC’s Associate Vice Provost for Technology Enhanced Learning and Associate CIO for USC’s Information Technology Services. She holds faculty appointments in the Roski School and in the USC Rossier School of Education and the USC Annenberg School of Communication and Journalism.

Susan offers over 20 years executive-level, higher education leadership experience and 35 years of university faculty experience. Her IT leadership portfolio includes CIO experience and oversight of both information and educational technology organizations, operations and services. She has deep knowledge in the areas of academic innovation, educational technology, learning space design, web services, and user experience. She is an expert on the full continuum of online learning from conceptualizing a program to launching a degree.
